About Electronic ARTrium

One of the Vertically Integrated Project (VIP) teams at Georgia Tech, which are interdisciplinary teams comprised of different levels of students from undergraduate sophomores to PhD students. THe team was split into split into four different subteams:

  • Electromechanical (EM)
  • Sensor Processing and Networking (SPN)
  • Visual Art and Sound Design (VASD)
  • Web and Social Media Design (WSMD)

About "Bee My Guide" - The ARTrium's Second Show

This is the ARTrium's second exhibit which focuses on:

  • integrating art and engineering
  • user interaction with the exhibit doesn't involve any wearable technology
  • having a full narrative to support the cohesiveness of the different sections of the exhibit
Players were taken through four sections where he/she would gather a number clue in each of the first three sections and input the code in the final section to help Mr.Bee unlock the door and return to his hive. Each section consisted of a game for the player to complete with the aid of a mechatronic animal.

My Work on the Project

Mechatronic Eye Tracking

The animatronic in Section Two was Cappy the Caterpillar who had eyes that had horizontal and vertical ranges of motion. In my first semester (Fall 2024) on the EM team, the assigned task was to develop a system for Cappy's eyes to be able to track the player based on the pose detection camera system that was being developed by the SPN team.
